# Israeli Tax Withholding Rates Reference

## Default Rates by Payment Type

These are the no-certificate ITA defaults. A valid withholding certificate
typically brings the rate down to 0-5%.

### Section 164, Income Payments
| Payment Type | Rate | Notes |
|-------------|------|-------|
| Services (individual, no certificate) | 30% | Consulting, freelance work; can reach ~47% for an unverified payee |
| Services (company, no certificate) | 20-30% | Varies by tax-office classification |
| Interest | 25% | Bank and non-bank interest |
| Dividends | 25% | Standard shareholders |
| Dividends (major shareholder) | 30% | Shareholders holding > 10% |
| Prizes and awards | 25% | Competition winnings, etc. |

The "20% for services" figure used by older guidance is wrong. With no
certificate the services default is 30%.

### Section 170, Special Payments
| Payment Type | Rate | Notes |
|-------------|------|-------|
| Rent (business/commercial property) | 35% | Commercial real estate |
| Rent (residential property) | 30% | Residential lettings |
| Royalties | 23% | Intellectual property payments |
| Payments to non-residents | 25% | Subject to tax treaty reduction |
| Insurance commissions | 20% | Agent commissions |
| Construction/service contractors | 30% | Building and renovation, no certificate |

## Thresholds

- **De-minimis floor:** a single payment to a payee below roughly 5,520 NIS (the
  annually-indexed threshold) does not require withholding, unless cumulative
  payments to that payee cross the threshold. Verify the current-year figure.
- **Mandatory-withholder turnover threshold:** a business becomes a mandatory
  withholding agent for service/asset payments once its turnover crosses the
  ITA's annually-indexed turnover threshold. Verify the current-year figure.

## Tax Treaty Reduced Rates (Common)
| Country | Dividends | Interest | Royalties |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| USA | 12.5-25% | 17.5% | 15% |
| UK | 15% | 15% | 15% |
| Germany | 10-25% | 15% | 5% |
| France | 10-15% | 10% | 10% |
| Canada | 15% | 15% | 15% |

NOTE: Treaty rates require proper documentation and forms.
Always verify specific treaty provisions.

## Withholding Certificate Rates
Businesses can apply for reduced rates:
- 0%, clean tax record, established business
- 2%, most common reduced rate
- 5%, standard reduced rate
- 10%, moderate reduction
- Custom rate based on tax assessment

## Reporting Forms
- **Form 102:** periodic deductions report and payment (monthly or bi-monthly),
  due the 15th of the month following the reporting period.
- **Form 856:** annual per-payee withholding reconciliation for supplier and
  service-provider payments, due April 30 of the following year.
